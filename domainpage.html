AGRICULTURAL HIGHER EDUCATION PROJECT (NAHEP)

Benificiares- Agricultural university students

Funded by world bank and Indian Governmet

The objective of the NAHEP for India is to support participating agricultural universities and ICAR in providing more relevant and higher quality education to Agricultural University students. In addition, a four-year degree in Agriculture, Horticulture, Fisheries and Forestry has been declared a professional degree. The objective of the NAHEP for India is to support participating agricultural universities and ICAR in providing more relevant and higher quality education to Agricultural University students1. The project aims to develop resources and mechanisms for supporting infrastructure, faculty and student advancement, and providing means for better governance and management of agricultural universities23. The project has three components: Component 1 - Support to Agricultural Universities; Component 2 - Investment in ICAR for Leadership in Agricultural Higher Education; Component 3 - Project Management and Learning23. The project would benefit all the Agricultural Universities (AUs), i.e. 63 State Agricultural Universities modelled on the US Land Grant University pattern, 5 Deemed to be Universities (DUs), three Central Agricultural University (CAUs) and four Central Universities (CUs) with Agriculture Faculty23. The project also supports various initiatives such as Career Development Centres, Policy Papers, Training Manuals, Agripreneurship, etc. for enhancing the employability and entrepreneurship skills of the students23.


PRADHAN MANTRI ANNANDATA FARMERS AAY SANRAKSAHN ABHIYAN (PM-AASHA)

Aimed at ensuring remunerative prices to the farmers for their produce. The umbrella scheme ‘PM-AASHA’ comprises three sub-schemes:

Price Support Scheme (PSS): The physical procurement of Pulses, Oilseeds and Copra will be done by Central Nodal Agencies (NAFED and FCI) with the proactive role of the state governments. Price Deficiency Payment Scheme (PDPS): it is proposed to cover all oilseeds for which Minimum support price (MSP) is notified. Stockist Scheme (PPPS): For oilseeds, the states will have the option to roll Procurement Stockist Scheme (PPSS) on pilot basis and Committee’s (APMC) Involving the participation of private stockiest. Selected private agency shall procure the commodity at MSP in the notified markets from the registered farmers, government to enter the market. The maximum service charges up to 15% of the notified MSP will be payable. The scheme was announced in September 2018 as an effort to ensure that farmers growing pulses, oilseeds etc actually got the minimum support prices they are promised for their crops each year2. The scheme comprises the erstwhile Price Support Scheme (PSS) with certain modifications and rolling out of new schemes of Price Deficiency Payment Scheme (PDPS) and pilot of Private Procurement and Stockist Scheme (PPSS)13. Under PM-AASHA, States/UTs are offered to choose either PSS and PDPS in a given procurement season with respect to particular oilseeds crop for the entire State3. The scheme also provides for a mechanism of additional procurement by private agencies under PPSS on pilot basis in selected districts of selected States3.
